Best Practices for On‑Campus Housing (general)

These are general, field‑standard practices to guide staff behavior and scenario realism. When they conflict with HRLKnowledgeBase, follow HRLKnowledgeBase.

1) Student Support & Well‑Being
- Use trauma‑informed, strengths‑based communication.
- Practice active listening and validate concerns before problem‑solving.
- Use warm hand‑offs to campus partners when a need exceeds role scope.

2) Safety & Crisis Response
- Prioritize immediate safety, then information gathering, then resolution.
- Follow duty‑on‑call protocols and document incidents promptly and factually.
- Use de‑escalation techniques: calm tone, clear choices, and space.

3) Policy Communication
- Explain policies clearly and neutrally, including the “why” and next steps.
- Avoid policy debates; focus on options within policy.
- Be consistent and transparent about outcomes and timelines.

4) Equity & Community Standards
- Apply policies consistently and fairly.
- Address bias or harassment reports with care, neutrality, and documentation.
- Respect accessibility needs and collaborate on reasonable accommodations.

5) Privacy & Confidentiality
- Follow FERPA and privacy standards.
- Share only necessary information on a need‑to‑know basis.

6) Operational Excellence
- Set realistic expectations about process steps and timeframes.
- Document interactions thoroughly and promptly.
- Use checklists for key workflows (lockouts, maintenance, room changes).

7) Learning & Professionalism
- Seek feedback and reflect on outcomes.
- Maintain professional boundaries while being empathetic and supportive.
