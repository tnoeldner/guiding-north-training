Guiding NORTH Framework & Training Context

Purpose: This document serves as the ground truth for the "Guiding NORTH" communication standard used by the University of North Dakota Housing & Residence Life. Use this context to grade responses, generate scenarios, or rewrite drafts.

1. The Guiding NORTH Framework

N - Navigate Needs (Listen & Understand)

Core Idea: Listen first to understand the real issue, not just the surface question.

Key Behaviors: Ask clarifying questions; validate feelings/perspective first; explain the "why" behind policies.

O - Own the Outcome (Responsibility & Resolution)

Core Idea: Take personal responsibility for getting the student to their destination.

Key Behaviors: The receiver owns the inquiry until resolved; warm handoffs only (no bouncing); follow up to confirm resolution.

R - Respond Respectfully (Tone & Professionalism)

Core Idea: Tone and language build trust and confidence.

Key Behaviors: Warm, professional, solution-focused tone; no jargon (speak plainly); maintain composure during conflict.

T - Timely & Truthful (Swift & Reliable)

Core Idea: Guidance is swift and reliable.

Key Behaviors: 24-hour response rule; verify accuracy before speaking (Single Source of Truth); honesty about delays.

H - Help Proactively (Anticipate & Prepare)

Core Idea: Anticipate the terrain ahead and clear the path.

Key Behaviors: Clarify "next steps"; use FAQs/Guides; identify systemic improvements.

3. Staff Roles & Expectations

Front Desk Staff

Description: Student employee or entry-level staff.

Expectations: Focus on warmth, accurate info, basic policy explanation, and warm handoffs. NOT expected to make policy exceptions.

Resident Director (RD)

Description: Live-in professional staff.

Expectations: Focus on de-escalation, conflict mediation, adjudication, and policy enforcement. Owns outcome of complex issues.

Assistant Director

Description: Senior leadership.

Expectations: Focus on high-level crisis management, angry parent relations, legal/liability concerns, and systemic solutions.

Facilities/Maintenance

Description: Repair and custodial staff.

Expectations: Focus on realistic timelines, explaining "why" regarding delays, and respecting privacy.

4. Operational Context & Policies (The "Truthful" Standard)

Front Desk Hours: 8:00 AM - 10:00 PM (M-F), 12:00 PM - 10:00 PM (Sat-Sun).

After Hours Protocol: Voicemails left after 10 PM are reviewed the next business morning. Staff are not expected to answer at 3 AM, but must acknowledge the delay the next morning.

Lockout Fees: $10 fee during business hours, $25 after hours.

Lost Keys: $75 core change fee (billed to student account).

Room Changes: Freeze period in effect for first 2 weeks of every semester (no moves allowed).
